How to register a Company in Nepal

Do you want to set up a public company or a private company?

Private companies are those where the number of shareholder(s) can start from 1 but cannot exceed 50. They cannot invite the general public for share subscription. The public companies are the ones with a minimum of 7 shareholders and a minimum paid-up capital of NPR 10 million. They may offer shares to the public at the Nepal Stock Exchange under the Company Act and Securities Act. A company carrying on business in banking, financial transactions, insurance-related transactions, a stock exchange, pension or mutual fund, or any other related business or transactions must be incorporated as a public limited company.

  • If you want to set up a company, first you will need to register your company’s name at the website of the Office of Company Registrar. Visit this link to create an account and request the reservation of a unique name for your company.
  • After approval has been acquired, you will need to approach the Office of Company Registrar and:
    • Submit an application form along with an NPR 10 stamp.
    • Two copies of Articles of Association.
    • Two copies of Memorandum of Association.
    • Attested copies of citizenship certificates of founder shareholders.
    • Attested copies of the agreement between the founder shareholders.
  • If the owner is another company, you will require the following documents:
    • Owning company’s registration certificate.
    • The owning company’s board decision to establish the proposed company.
    • Related documents of the company, such as Articles of Association and Memorandum of Association.
    • An appointment letter of the company’s representative in the proposed company.
  • If the company has foreign investors:
    • Approval of foreign investment from the related authority.
    • If the foreign investor is an individual, then a copy of the investor’s passport.
    • If the foreign investor is a company, then related documents of the company such as Articles of Association and Memorandum of Association.
  • Registration fees will depend on the size of the capital investment.
